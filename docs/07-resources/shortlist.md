# 07.1 — Resource Shortlist (High-Signal Only)

> Verified via web search, July 2026. The rule: **a few great sources worked through beats a hundred bookmarked.** Everything here earns its place. The "avoid" section at the bottom is just as important — it saves you the time and money beginners waste.

## Tier 0 — Start here (this week)

1. **Meta Blueprint** — Meta's own free training. 90+ courses. Not exciting, but it's the ground truth for *how the platform actually works* and it's free. Do the paid-ads fundamentals. → [facebook.com/business/learn](https://www.facebook.com/business/learn)
2. **Meta Ad Library** — the single best free creative resource on earth. Search any brand, see every ad they're running. Ads running a *long time* = winners (they don't keep losers live). Build a swipe habit here. → [facebook.com/ads/library](https://www.facebook.com/ads/library/)
3. ***$100M Offers* — Alex Hormozi** — the book on offer construction, the #1-leverage skill ([why](../00-roadmap/skills-gap-analysis.md#1-offer--positioning-design-)). Reads like a spec doc; an engineer will love it. Pair with *$100M Leads* for lead-gen funnels.

## Tier 1 — Core operators to learn from (paid social, DTC)

4. **Common Thread Collective / the DTC newsletter + "Statlas"** — CTC tie creative and paid social back to *financial forecasting* (growth accounting). The most rigorous, economics-first voice in ecommerce growth — exactly the lens an engineer should adopt. → [commonthreadco.com](https://www.commonthreadco.com/)
5. **Nick Shackelford / Structured** — spent $200M+ on Meta; his agency spends ~$15M/mo. Follow his breakdowns of account structure and creative for *current* tactical reality (LinkedIn + podcasts). Structured is the most-cited Meta agency in DTC right now.
6. **Andrew Faris (AJF Growth / "The Andrew Faris Podcast")** — sharp on the two-stage creative scaling model (find what resonates → scale into diverse assets) and profit-focused media buying. Deep, no-fluff thinking.
7. **Demand Curve — the Growth Newsletter** — startup/solo-founder-oriented growth tactics, well-edited, actionable. Good breadth across the funnel, not just ads. → [demandcurve.com/newsletters](https://www.demandcurve.com/newsletters)

## Tier 2 — Depth when you're ready

8. **CXL** — rigorous, experiment-driven paid + CRO courses (their flagship Meta ads course ~$299). "Think like a growth operator": validation systems, scalable structures, analytical control. Invest here *after* you've shipped and have context to hang it on. → [cxl.com](https://cxl.com/)
9. **The "Dollar-A-Day" approach** — the discipline of validating hooks/creatives/audiences on tiny budgets before scaling. Perfectly matched to a solo learner's budget; embodies the [90-day plan's](../00-roadmap/90-day-plan.md) test-small philosophy.
10. **Copywriting foundations** — study proven frameworks (PAS, AIDA, the 4 U's) plus a swipe file of ads you admire. Don't buy an expensive course; read *The Boron Letters* (Gary Halbert, free online) and rewrite ads daily. Copy is a meta-skill ([why](../00-roadmap/skills-gap-analysis.md#6-copywriting-persuasion-in-words)).

## Overrated / avoid (save your money and attention)

- **$2,000+ "Facebook Ads mastery" guru courses.** The high-priced course-selling industry mostly teaches outdated interest-stacking and recycled basics. Meta Blueprint (free) + operators above + *actually spending money* teaches more. If a course's own ads scream "make $10k/mo," run.
- **"Secret audiences / winning interests" content.** Dead as of Jan 15, 2026 — Meta removed granular interest targeting. Anyone selling interest-stacking secrets is selling 2019. Targeting is automated now; creative is the lever.
- **Generic "social media marketing" / influencer-guru content.** Vanity-metric theater (followers, engagement, "going viral"). You want *direct-response* and *paid acquisition*, a different discipline. Ignore "grow your personal brand" advice — it's not your goal.
- **Endless podcast/YouTube consumption as "learning."** Consumption feels productive and moves nothing. Cap passive intake; every hour of study should attach to a thing you're about to ship. Re-read the [ship-over-study rule](../00-roadmap/90-day-plan.md#rules-for-the-whole-90-days).
- **Over-investing in tools before you have data.** Triple Whale, Motion, etc. are great *at scale*; at your stage they're premature. You'll build your own [dashboard](../06-automation/01-reporting-dashboard.md) anyway.
- **Attribution-tool rabbit holes early.** Perfect attribution is impossible and obsessing over it is a beginner time-sink. Get CAPI to "good" (EMQ 8.0+) and move on.
