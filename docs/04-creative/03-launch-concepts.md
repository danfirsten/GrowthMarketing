# 04.3 — Launch Creative Concepts (Campaign #1)

> The first 5 creative *concepts* (distinct ideas, not variants) for the launch campaign. Each tests a different **angle** — a different reason to care — so the algorithm and the data tell us which audience/motivation actually responds. In 2026, this IS the targeting. Production notes assume solo-dev resources: screen recordings + phone footage + Canva/CapCut. Done > pretty.

**Constants held across all 5 (clean test):** offer (free signup, no card), landing page, broad Advantage+ Audience, optimization event (`CompleteRegistration`). **Variable: the concept.**

**Compliance guardrails (all concepts):** no grade promises/guarantees ("study faster" ✅, "get an A" ❌), no "you struggle with X" personal-attribute phrasing, nothing implying Meta knows the viewer is a student.

---

## C1 — "60 Seconds" (speed demo · the banker)

- **Angle:** raw speed/magic of the transformation. **Audience it finds:** everyone; broadest.
- **Format:** 9:16 screen-recording Reel, 15–20s.
- **Hook (first 3s):** screen shows a 2-hour lecture on YouTube; text overlay: **"your 2-hour lecture → full study kit in 60s"**.
- **Body:** timelapse: paste link → tabs light up one by one (notes, flashcards, quiz, study guide) → flick through real artifacts. End card: "Free — first kit in 60 seconds." CTA → signup.
- **Why it might win:** demo IS the proof; zero acting ability required; the product's strongest 15 seconds.

## C2 — "Lock In" (finals-energy slang · culture)

- **Angle:** the moment of resolve before studying. **Audience it finds:** crammers, finals-mode undergrads.
- **Format:** 9:16 UGC-style selfie video or meme-format static.
- **Hook:** **"POV: it's finals week. Time to lock in."**
- **Body:** cut from the POV to the screen: lecture pasted, kit generated, review session running. Overlay: "locking in just got a lot easier." CTA: "Lock in free."
- **Why it might win:** native-language hook = doesn't feel like an ad; strong thumb-stop with the right meme-face opener.

## C3 — "Stop pasting lectures into ChatGPT" (vs. the real competitor · switcher)

- **Angle:** you're already doing this the hard way. **Audience it finds:** AI-native students (highest intent — they already believe in AI studying).
- **Format:** 9:16 talking-head or split-screen comparison, 20–30s.
- **Hook:** **"Stop pasting your lectures into ChatGPT."**
- **Body:** left: ChatGPT wall-of-text, re-prompting, copy-paste chaos. Right: one paste → structured kit with flashcards that schedule themselves. "One paste. The whole kit. It remembers when you should review."
- **Why it might win:** intercepts an existing behavior (cheapest conversion in marketing is redirecting intent, not creating it).

## C4 — "You'll forget 90% by Friday" (retention science · problem-first)

- **Angle:** the forgetting curve — watching ≠ learning. **Audience it finds:** conscientious students, grad/professional preppers (the high-LTV segment).
- **Format:** 1:1 or 9:16 static (or simple animated curve), strong copy carry.
- **Hook:** **"You watched the whole lecture. By Friday you'll remember ~10% of it."**
- **Body:** one-line science (forgetting curve), then: "Flashcards on a schedule that interrupts forgetting — generated from *your* lecture, automatically. Same algorithm as Anki (SM-2)." CTA: "Make it stick — free."
- **Why it might win:** problem-first hooks out-pull feature hooks when awareness is low; credibility (Anki/SM-2) resonates with serious studiers who pay and stay.

## C5 — "TA in your pocket" (companion/brand · name-aligned)

- **Angle:** it's not a tool, it's your TA. **Audience it finds:** students who feel unsupported (big lectures, online courses).
- **Format:** 9:16 UGC testimonial-style, 20s.
- **Hook:** **"I basically have a TA in my pocket now."**
- **Body:** casual confession-cam: "It takes the lecture, makes the notes, the flashcards, quizzes me before exams... like office hours, whenever." Show 2–3s of product. CTA: "Meet your pocket TA — free."
- **Why it might win:** builds the brand while it sells; if this angle wins, the name compounds every future ad. ⚠️ Testimonial framing must be honest — script it as *your own* founder usage ("I built this because...") until real user quotes exist.

## C6 — "You skipped class. Your TA didn't." (skipped-class angle · founder's pick)

- **Angle:** you skipped the lecture (or zoned out, or watched none of the recordings) — and you can still be ready. Guilt-relief. **Audience it finds:** the huge silent segment that's behind on lectures; crammers with a backlog.
- **Origin:** user's line — *"Just because you skipped class doesn't mean you have to miss class."* Sharpened for a 3-second hook (the skip/miss echo reads as a riddle on first pass; hooks must resolve instantly).
- **Format:** 9:16 Reel; opens on a full "Lecture 1–17 (unwatched)" playlist — instantly relatable dread.
- **Hook:** **"You skipped the lecture. Your TA didn't."**
- **Body:** paste the recording → kit generates → flick through notes/flashcards. Overlay: "17 unwatched lectures → 17 study kits." CTA: "Catch up free."
- **Alt hooks to test as variants if the angle wins:** "Went to zero lectures. Ready for the final anyway." · "Skip class, not the material." · the original long line as landing-page sub-headline.
- **Why it might win:** names a behavior nobody admits but everybody does; strong guilt-relief emotion; name-aligned (the TA showed up for you).
- **⚠️ Positioning guardrail:** this is an *ad angle*, not the brand tagline — branding the whole product around skipping class narrows out the serious/high-LTV preppers (C4's audience) and invites the "AI = cutting corners" frame with schools. Run it as one concept among six; let the data decide how big this segment is.

---

## Production checklist (target: all 6 shot/assembled in one weekend)
- [ ] 1 clean screen-recording session of the real product on a real lecture (fuels C1, C3, C5, C6 b-roll)
- [ ] Phone selfie footage: C2 POV, C5 confession-cam (you, or a student friend on camera); unwatched-playlist screenshot for C6
- [ ] Statics for C4 (+ a static fallback of C1: before/after screenshot) in Canva
- [ ] Captions burned in (85%+ watch muted), first frame readable as a still
- [ ] Each ad URL tagged `utm_content=c1…c5` ([tracking spec](../05-analytics/02-pixel-and-capi.md))

## How we'll read results (per [experiment file](../08-experiments/2026-08-launch-campaign-01.md))
- **Hook rate** (3s views ÷ impressions) ≥ ~25% = hook works · **CTR** ≥ ~1% = promise works · **cost per signup** = the verdict.
- Kill the bottom concepts, make 3 variants of the winner's *angle*, repeat. That's the engine.
