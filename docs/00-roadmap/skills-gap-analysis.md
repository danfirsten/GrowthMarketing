# 00.1 — Skills Gap Analysis

> Given your profile — strong engineer, zero marketing reps — here are the skills to build, ranked by **leverage** (impact per hour invested). "Time to dangerous" = how long until you're competent enough to get real results, not mastery.

## Your starting position (honest audit)

**Strengths you can weaponize immediately:**
- Systems thinking, APIs, automation, data handling, willingness to measure. Most marketers are weak here. This is your moat.
- Comfort with feedback loops and iteration — the core rhythm of paid growth.

**Gaps, and the trap:**
- Zero reps on the *human* side: what makes someone stop scrolling, want a thing, and click "buy." You cannot code your way to this — only reps and study.
- **The trap:** you will be tempted to spend your time building tools and dashboards (comfortable, feels productive) instead of writing ads and offers (uncomfortable, high-leverage). Notice this urge and resist it. Build systems to *serve* the marketing, not to *avoid* it.

## The skills, ranked by leverage

### 1. Offer & positioning design 🥇
**What it is:** Constructing an irresistible offer — the thing you're actually selling, its price, bonuses, risk-reversal (guarantee), and the *promise*. Positioning = who it's for and why it's different.

**Why it's #1:** It's the biggest multiplier in the whole funnel ([foundations map](../01-foundations/growth-marketing-map.md#where-the-leverage-actually-is-ranked)). A great offer forgives a mediocre ad; a great ad can't save a weak offer. It's also the most *ignored* skill because it's not a "channel."

**Time to dangerous:** 1–2 weeks of focused study + one real offer built.
**Compounds with engineering:** You can codify offers as structured data, A/B test offer variants programmatically, and template them. Study Alex Hormozi's *$100M Offers* — it's practically a spec document.

### 2. Creative strategy & production 🥈
**What it is:** Hooks (first 3 seconds), angles (the *reason* the product matters to *this* person), formats (UGC, static, testimonial, demo), and a system for producing and testing lots of them.

**Why it's #2 and rising:** As of Jan 15, 2026 Meta removed granular interest targeting — **creative is now the targeting mechanism.** The algorithm finds your buyers *based on who responds to your creative.* Whoever produces the most good, varied creative wins. This is now the primary paid-social skill.

**Time to dangerous:** 2–4 weeks to write competent hooks/angles; ongoing forever to build taste.
**Compounds with engineering:** *Enormously.* AI-assisted creative pipelines, brand guides as structured data feeding generation, automated variant production, creative testing harnesses. This is where your edge is largest — see [06-automation](../06-automation/). Top brands ship 200–300 concepts/month; systematize toward that.

### 3. Funnel & conversion design 🥉
**What it is:** Landing pages, lead magnets, email sequences, upsells — turning a click into a customer and a customer into a repeat customer. Message match, friction reduction, proof, and copywriting.

**Why it's #3:** Every point of landing-page conversion rate directly divides your CAC. It's high-leverage and *very* buildable.

**Time to dangerous:** 2–3 weeks to build a competent landing page + 4-email welcome sequence.
**Compounds with engineering:** You can build pages/funnels far faster and more custom than any no-code marketer. Danger: don't over-engineer — a great offer on a plain page beats a beautiful page with a weak offer.

### 4. Analytics, tracking & attribution
**What it is:** Pixel + Conversions API (CAPI), event match quality, CAC/LTV measurement, attribution windows, and the judgment to read data and decide **kill vs. scale**.

**Why it's #4 (not higher):** It's a *prerequisite* and a genuine edge for you, but it's an enabler — it tells you what's working, it doesn't *make* things work. Get it "good enough" early, then let it mature.

**Time to dangerous:** 1 week for pixel+CAPI setup (easy for you); the *judgment* (reading noisy data without fooling yourself) takes months of reps.
**Compounds with engineering:** Massively — this is literally software. CAPI, server-side tracking, dashboards, automated reports. Most marketers pay agencies thousands for what you can build. Your **strongest** technical moat.

### 5. Media buying & campaign operation
**What it is:** Actually operating Meta Ads Manager — campaign structure, budgets, the learning phase, scaling rules, reading the account daily, killing and iterating.

**Why it's #5:** In 2026 Meta's AI does much of what media buyers used to do manually. The remaining skill is *structure, budget discipline, and knowing when to leave it alone* vs. intervene. Important, but less than beginners think.

**Time to dangerous:** 1 week to launch competently; discipline (not over-tinkering) is the hard part and takes reps.
**Compounds with engineering:** Automated rules, budget scripts via the Marketing API, alerting. Later-stage automation.

### 6. Copywriting (persuasion in words)
**What it is:** Writing that makes people feel and act — ad copy, headlines, email, page copy. Cuts across #1–#3.

**Why it's a "meta-skill":** It's not a channel; it's the medium of everything above. Worth deliberate study because it's the skill an engineer is *least* practiced at and it silently gates offer, creative, and funnel quality.

**Time to dangerous:** Ongoing. Study frameworks (PAS, AIDA, the 4 U's), then write daily. Fastest way to improve: rewrite ads you see, and study swipe files.
**Compounds with engineering:** AI-assisted drafting *if* you have the taste to judge outputs. Taste first, automation second.

### 7. Data-driven experimentation & growth process
**What it is:** Running a disciplined test loop — hypotheses, sample sizes, avoiding false positives, systematic iteration. The scientific method applied to growth.

**Why it's listed:** You likely *already have* most of this from engineering. Named explicitly so you apply it and don't fall for vanity metrics or noise. This is the skill that *ties the others together* into compounding progress.

**Time to dangerous:** You're mostly there. Reframe your existing rigor onto marketing metrics.

## The compounding thesis

Your engineering skill doesn't just *add* to these — it *multiplies* the ones that are buildable (creative production, funnels, tracking, media-buying automation). But it multiplies **zero** on the two purely human skills (offer/positioning and creative *judgment/taste*). So:

- **Spend LEARNING time** on the human skills: offer (#1), creative taste (#2), copywriting (#6). These are your real gaps.
- **Spend BUILDING time** on the buildable skills: tracking (#4), creative *production* pipelines (#2), funnels (#3), media-buying automation (#5).
- **Apply your existing rigor** (#7) to everything.

> The engineer who learns taste beats the marketer who learns to code, because taste is the slower thing to acquire — start it now.

## Priority for the next 90 days

Focus order for the [90-day plan](90-day-plan.md): **Offer → Creative → Funnel → Tracking → Launch → Iterate.** Media buying and copywriting develop *through* doing the above, not as separate study blocks.
